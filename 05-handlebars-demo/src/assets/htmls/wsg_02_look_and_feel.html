Look and Feel

Familiarize yourself with the brand, the use of colours, the font usage and our general look and feel..

Introduction

This web site forms the basis of the graphic identity or web style guide of KIKLOS. This document defines the visual identity of the brand, especially in the context of the Committee of Regions. This web site aims to go further into the specifics of Internet media. This style guide defines the rules for any type of public communication using the web. It also applies to the standard definitions of the framework Twitter Bootstrap version 3.0.

Brand Elements

Logotype

The logo of KIKLOS is the expression of its personality and its values. Official, bilingual and unique, it can, in no way, suffer from any modifications or alterations, to be always recognized. The custom typography, colors, layout, movement... nothing has been chosen randomly. As such, it is essential to give this logo permanently all the attention and respect of graphic rules that will always show it to his advantage.


To ensure the readability of the baseline, resizing should always be homothetic and meet a minimum width of 64px. In the case of an available width less than 64px, the logo must be deprived of its « KIKLOS » Your Opinion Matters.

Favicon & icon touch

Favicon

The favicon is an icon that illustrates a web site. The term comes from the contraction of the words "favorite" and "icon". Web browsers use this icon in the title bar, bookmarks, tabs and other shortcuts. The dimensions of the favicon are generally a square of 16 or 32 pixels.

64px 48px 32px 16px
Touch icon

On mobile devices, a site can be added to the home screen as an application. The touch icon is the illustration of the web site. Led by graphic assumption dictated by Apple & iOS, the touch icon often features rounded corners and shading effects or reflections, without this being an obligation.

By definition, a touch icon must be unique per application and offer an ideal represantation of the web site it represents. The logo must stay ahead and legible. It must be used in accordance with its rules of employment.

Logotype colors

#1d3751; /* dark blue */

#ee3853; /* red */

Dark blue and red are the basic colors of the identity of KIKLOS. They are united in the logo which reflects the brand identity: blue symbol of reliability and red as a symbol for dynamism. When it applies, blue color should be used sparingly because heavy use of this specific color would not differentiate fairly enough KIKLOS from its competitors. Red brings freshness and dynamism to the visual communication. This color will be used to build a fresh image of the institution and accentuate modernity.
