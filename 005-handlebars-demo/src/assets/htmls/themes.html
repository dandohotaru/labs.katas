Themes

Agriculture & maritime and consumer policies

The farming sector in Europe employs almost 30 million people and rural areas account for 90% of the EU’s entire territory and are home to 60% of its citizens. The Committee of the Regions (CoR) pays considerable attention to agriculture policies and the long-term protection of Europe’s rural areas. It also examines issues such as food and food safety, GMOs, animal welfare and plant health, biodiversity, health, fisheries and maritime affairs.

Cohesion policy

Regional and urban policies help to strengthen economic, social and territorial cohesion in the European Union by reducing development disparities between regions. EU cohesion policy works towards reducing disparities by continually improving competiveness and employment and is a powerful tool for solidarity in the EU.

14 opinions

Constitutional & interinstitunial affairs

The CoR promotes the implementation of the principles included in the Charter for Multilevel Governance in Europe adopted in 2014. The CoR monitors the EU Regulatory Fitness Programme (REFIT) to improve the quality of EU legislation, and analyses the application of the principle of Subsidiarity in the EU.

79 opinions

Economics & Finance

The generation of economic growth and job creation happens primarily at local and regional levels. Due to the expertise and competence of local and regional authorities in the field of employment and growth, their involvement in the policy making process is of vital importance. They must also be given the necessary resources to promote smart, sustainable and inclusive growth in their territories.

175 opinions

Education and Culture

The CoR follows developments in the fostering of inclusion and equality through investment in education, vocational training and skills leading to upward social convergence. The CoR also promotes the role of education in combating youth radicalisation.

A new policy framework is being developed for a true EU Cultural Diplomacy. The CoR supports greater visibility for high level cultural relations in foreign affairs and contributes to enhancing the role of Europe's cities and regions in international diplomacy.

14 opinions

Energy

The delivery of policies and actions necessary to protect our energy supply and to create a competitive energy sector takes place primarily in the cities and regions. Through the CoR, the local and regional authorities of the EU have a strong voice in the decision-making process in these areas.

14 opinions

Enterprises & Industries

The growth of the EU economy is based on local and regional drivers. Support for research and innovation, digital economy, enterprise policy including SMEs and completing the single market are the among the main concerns of the CoR, as many of the projects funded from the EU Structural and Investment Funds will be carried out with a strong involvement of EU local and regional authorities.

14 opinions

Environment

Cities and regions are at the forefront of the crucial work of delivering policies to protect our environment. Local and regional authorities are instrumental in creating a sustainable Europe when it comes to tackling climate change and reducing air pollution.

56 opinions

External Relations, enlargement and neighbourhood policy

The CoR cooperates with local and regional authorities in the EU neighbouring countries to forge partnerships and share knowledge and good practices across borders. It has set up several international forums such as the Euro-Mediterranean Regional and Local Assembly (ARLEM) and the Conference of the Regional and Local Authorities for the Eastern Partnership (CORLEAP). The CoR has also established joint consultative committees and working groups to promote political dialogue with local and regional authorities in accession countries.

14 opinions

Justice & Home Affairs

The CoR has a vital interest in Justice and Home Affairs (JHA) policies, which are a basis for the protection and promotion of fundamental rights and freedoms in the EU. Cities and regions are primary actors in dealing with migration and its consequences, and the CoR supports a common EU policy in this area, based on solidarity and shared responsibility between the EU, the Member States and regional and local authorities.

28 opinions

Social policies

The CoR supports a strong social dimension in EU policy-making, notably in economic policy and in the promotion of equal opportunities and stimulating youth employment. The CoR is committed to contributing to initiatives that support EU citizens.

14 opinions

Transport

A sound transport infrastructure is fundamental to a smooth-running internal market, the movement of people and goods, and the economic, social and territorial cohesion of the European Union. Rail, sea, air and road transport policy is a central concern for local and regional authorities throughout Europe.
