Commissions

CIVEX

Citizenship, Governance, Institutional and External Affairs
CIVEX provides the secretariat for the Conference of Regional and Local Authorities for the Eastern Partnership (CORLEAP), the Euro-Mediterranean Regional and Local Assembly (ARLEM), the Joint Consultative Committees (JCC) and the Working Groups (WG) with and on the enlargement countries, the Ukraine Task Force (TF) and the Subsidiarity Monitoring Network. It provides the necessary resources and instruments for the Committee of the Regions to fully play its advisory role in the EU legislative and political process in the above-mentioned fields.
The CIVEX members and the members of CORLEAP, ARLEM, the JCCs, the WGs and the TF Ukraine, supported by the CIVEX commission secretariat, draft opinions and reports, follow up and monitor their impact after adoption, present the CoR or partner position on CIVEX work remits in debates and conferences, and meet regularly at commission and CoR plenary level or with their counterparts in enlargement and neighbourhood countries to discuss, exchange views and practices, and adopt opinions and reports.
In addition to supporting the members' work, the CIVEX commission secretariat is responsible for organising the meetings, hearings and external events of the CIVEX commission and of CORLEAP, ARLEM, the JCCs, the WGs, and the Ukraine TF on decentralised cooperation and of the Subsidiarity Steering Group (SSG) and Subsidiarity Expert Group (SEG) in the relevant policy fields. It also manages the Subsidiarity Monitoring Network; monitoring the principles of subsidiarity and proportionality throughout the EU policy and law-making process, and for coordinating CoR participation in the REFIT platform.

COTER

Territorial Cohesion Policy and EU Budget
The COTER Working Group on the EU Budget supports the work of the COTER commission in the following fields: annual EU budget; multiannual financial framework; and local and regional finances.
COTER prepares and provides the necessary resources and instruments for the Committee to fully play its advisory role in the EU legislative and political process.
The COTER commission coordinates the framing of the political messages of the European Committee of the Regions in the following areas: economic, social and territorial cohesion; the Structural Funds; spatial planning; urban policy; housing; transport, including TEN-T; macro-regions; territorial cooperation; and regional statistics and indicators. The COTER Working Group on the EU Budget supports the work of the COTER commission in the following fields: annual EU budget; multiannual financial framework; and local and regional finances.
The COTER secretariat assists the members in drawing up COTER opinions, supporting the political process within the Bureau and the Plenary Session, and is responsible for helping with follow-up and impact evaluation activities. The secretariat organises commission meetings and external events in the relevant policy fields.
In addition, COTER is responsible for managing the Platform of European Groupings for Territorial Cooperation (EGTC) and for collecting good practices and exchanging experiences among local and regional authorities on the collaboration of partner regions and cities. COTER also provides guidance on and runs Territorial Impact Assessments (TIA), strengthening the evidence base of the CoR's political messages about the effect of EU legislation at the local level.

ECON

Economic Policy
The Commission for Economic Policy (ECON) prepares and provides the necessary resources and instruments for the Committee to fully play its advisory role in the EU legislative and political process.
It supports the framing of the political messages of the Committee of the Regions in the fields of: industrial policy; SME policy; economic and monetary policy; economic governance, the European Semester and Europe 2020; the internal market; international trade and tariffs; and competition and state aid policy.
The ECON commission secretariat assists the members in drawing up ECON opinions, supporting the political process within the Bureau and Plenary Session, and is responsible for helping with follow-up and impact evaluation activities. It organises commission meetings and external events in the relevant policy fields.
ECON is responsible for managing the CoR's Europe 2020 Monitoring Platform and for collecting good practices and exchanging experiences among local and regional authorities on the implementation of the strategy on the ground. It also provides guidance on and runs consultations and online surveys among local and regional practitioners, strengthening the analytical and evidence base of the CoR's political messages.
The team is also responsible for the annual European Entrepreneurial Regions (EER) Award initiative.

ENVE

Environment, Climate Change and Energy
The ENVE commission secretariat prepares and provides high-quality support to the members on all content-related, political and procedural issues in the policy areas for which ENVE is responsible. The secretariat coordinates the strategic planning, implementation and follow-up of this consultative work for the other EU institutions and closely monitors its impact.
Its secretariat assists members in drawing up ENVE opinions, supporting the political process within the Bureau and Plenary Session, and is responsible for preparing follow-up and impact evaluation activities.
It organises commission meetings and external events in the relevant policy fields.

175 opinions

NAT

Natural Resources
The NAT commission secretariat delivers high-quality advice to the members of the NAT commission on all content-related, political and procedural questions in the policy areas for which NAT is responsible. The secretariat is responsible for ensuring good follow-up to all policy recommendations made by NAT members to the EU institutions and for preparing the strategic planning of the work of the NAT commission to seek to achieve the required impact in the key policy areas.
The NAT secretariat assists members with drawing up NAT opinions, supporting the political process within the Bureau and Plenary Session, and is responsible for preparing the follow-up and impact evaluation activities.

14 opinions

SEDEC

Social Policy, Education, Employment, Research and Culture
The Commission for Social Policy, Education, Employment, Research and Culture (SEDEC) prepares and provides the necessary resources and instruments for the Committee to fully play its advisory role in the EU legislative and political process.
The SEDEC commission supports the drawing up of the Committee of the Regions' political messages in the fields of: employment and social policy (Social); education, culture, youth, sport and multilingualism (Education); innovation, R&D and smart specialisation (Innovation); the digital agenda, ICT networks and the information society (Digital); and audiovisual and media-related technologies (Audiovisual).
The SEDEC secretariat assists members in drawing up SEDEC opinions, supporting the political process within the Bureau and Plenary Session, and is responsible for helping with follow-up and impact evaluation activities.
The secretariat organises commission meetings and external events in the relevant policy fields.
It is also responsible for managing the research programme/studies of Directorate C (COTER, ECON and SEDEC commissions).
